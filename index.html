A Little Bit About Me

I'm a Research Engineer with experience in Microfluidic design, MEMS fabrication, Software development, Numerical Simulations (FEA) and Molecular biology. I am currently working at Ontera, Inc. My role here is fairly multidisciplianry; always challenging and interesting. I enjoy designing and building anything from software systems to a hand-held Point-of-care diagnostic solution which could make it to the shelves of Walgreens OR CVS tomorrow and make a difference in human lives.

I pursued Electrical Engineering with a focus on Advanced Materials and Devices. As an academic researcher at the Integrative Biosensing Laboratory (IBL), I carried out research in the area of Solid-state Nanopores based Biosensors. My research in the lab helped me learn about the underlying biophysics and electrokinetic phenomenon that govern molecular transport in these sensors. It brought me to the sunny state of California, where my current emplyer, a leader in Nanopore development has been steadfast in improving this technology, with applications in both life sciences and agricultural genomics.

As part of all my projects, I have enjoyed contributing to a software systems propelling these devices. I bring my experience from yesteryears to good use when it comes to programming microcontrollers that govern the fluidic movement in devices or programming smart data analysis modules for large raw current data from amplifiers, for instance.

Check out the projects I have contributed to. Feel free to reach out if you'd like to learn more.
