A Little Bit About Me

I'm a Research Engineer with experience in Microfluidic design, MEMS fabrication, Software development, Numerical Simulations (FEA) and Molecular biology. I am currently working at Ontera, Inc. My role here is multidisciplianry; always challenging and interesting. I enjoy designing and building anything from software systems to a hand-held Point-of-care diagnostic device which could make it to the shelves of Walgreens OR CVS tomorrow and make a difference in human lives.

I pursued Electrical Engineering with a focus on Advanced Materials and Devices. As an academic researcher at the Integrative Biosensing Laboratory (IBL), I carried out research in the area of Solid-state Nanopores based Biosensors. My research in the lab helped me learn about the underlying biophysics and electrokinetic phenomenon that govern molecular transport in these sensors. It brought me to the sunny state of California, where my current emplyer, a leader in Nanopore development has been steadfast in improving this technology, with applications in both life sciences and agricultural genomics.

As part of all my projects, I have enjoyed contributing to a software systems propelling these devices. I bring my experience from yesteryears to good use when it comes to programming microcontrollers that govern the fluidic movement in devices or programming smart data analysis modules for large raw data from amplifiers, for instance.

Finite Element Analysis (FEA)

Mar 2015 - Nov 2018

About:COMSOL is a simulation environment, designed with real-world applications in mind. The point of all simulation of course is to mimic, as closely as possible, effects that are observed in reality. Be they in an engineering or scientific context. To do this, there is the need of multiphysics; that is multiple scientific models that include the things that you are interested in. Some of these are: acoustics, electromagnetics, chemical reactions, mechanics, fluid flow, and heat transfer. Because the real world includes all of these effects, your simulation environment should also.

Contribution: Studied the underlying forces governing electrokinetic transport in a nanopore, estimated surface charges, velocities of moving charged species. Designed a course around the tool and taought a laboratory oriented course at the graduate level.

A study on the choice of obstacle shapes and sizes in the microchannel impacting the mixing of fluids.

Jan 2016 - Jun 2016

About:Passive mixers rely on the channel geometry to mix fluids. However, many previously reported designs either work efficiently only at moderate to high Reynolds numbers (Re), or require a complex 3D channel geometry that is often difficult to fabricate. In this project, I reported 2 designs out of many permutaions that were simulated, for clean room fabrication and characterization of a planar passive microfluidic mixer capable of mixing at low Reynolds numbers. The design incorporates differnt shapes and locations of obstructions within the microchannel to break-up and recombine the flow. Simulation and experimental results of the developed micromixer show excellent mixing performance over a wide range of flow conditions

Contribution: Used CFD Geom and CFD Ace+ for simulating over a dozen designs with different post shapes, sizes and spacing in the microchannel designs (A and B). Chose the best performing one for clean room fabrication. Tested the fluid mixing performance and characterised it.

Skills Exercised:CFD analysis, Soft lithography, Photolithography, ImageJ, Microfluidics, Data Analysis, Python
